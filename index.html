Introduction

Nintendo Co., Ltd. is a Japanese multinational consumer electronics and video game company headquartered in Kyoto. It was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi.

History

1889–1932: Origin as a Playing Card Business

Founded in 1889 as Nintendo Karuta by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Japan, Nintendo began by crafting traditional Japanese playing cards, especially hanafuda. The company's name, often interpreted as "leave luck to heaven," underscored its early direction. Despite obstacles, including catering to a niche market and dealing with manufacturing complexities, Nintendo's success stemmed from producing accessible, lower-quality cards named Tengu. Over time, the company expanded globally and secured a pivotal partnership with Japan Tobacco in 1907, propelling it to become Japan's leading playing card business by 1929.

1933–1968: Incorportation, expansion, and diversification

From 1933 to 1968, Nintendo underwent significant transformations and expansions. The company evolved from a general partnership named Yamauchi Nintendo & Co., Ltd., establishing a new corporate headquarters in 1933. Hiroshi Yamauchi took over as president in 1950, introducing pivotal changes such as mass-producing plastic playing cards and automating production. Collaborating with The Walt Disney Company in 1959, Nintendo entered the children's market and diversified its offerings, marking its transition into manufacturing games alongside playing cards.

1969–1972: Classic and electronic toys

Between 1969 and 1972, Nintendo made significant strides in research and development under the leadership of Hiroshi Yamauchi. The company established a dedicated department for electronic devices, led by Gunpei Yokoi, resulting in the creation of popular classic tabletop games such as chess, shogi, go, and mahjong. A pivotal moment came in 1970 with the release of Japan's first electronic toy, the Beam Gun, followed by successful collaborations with Magnavox for light gun controllers and the launch of innovative toys like the Ultra Hand and Ultra Machine.

2017 - Present days

In the period spanning from 2017 to the present, Nintendo's influence on the gaming industry continued to evolve. The Nintendo Switch, a hybrid console combining home and handheld gaming, was released in 2017, featuring innovative Joy-Con controllers and a diverse game library from third-party developers. The release of the Nintendo Labo line in 2018 brought unique cardboard accessories to interact with the Switch. Leadership changes saw Shuntaro Furukawa becoming the company president in 2018, and in 2019, Doug Bowser taking over as the president of Nintendo of America. Strategic alliances with Tencent expanded Nintendo's presence in China in 2019. The company's expansion also extended beyond gaming, with the opening of Super Nintendo World at Universal Studios Japan in 2021 and the production of animated projects, including the highly successful Super Mario Bros. Movie in 2023.

Products

Nintendo Switch

The Nintendo Switch is a hybrid video game console, with the main unit inserted onto a docking station to connect to a television.

Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS is a handheld gaming console that offers 3D gaming without the need for special glasses.

The Super Mario Bros. game series

Featuring the adventures of Mario, a plumber turned hero, as he navigates through imaginative worlds to rescue Princess Peach from the clutches of the villainous Bowser, the series has defined platforming gameplay and cultural impact since its debut in 1985.
