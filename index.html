Introduction

Nintendo Co., Ltd. is a Japanese multinational consumer electronics and video game company headquartered in Kyoto. It was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi.

History

1889–1932: Origin as a Playing Card Business

Founded in 1889 as Nintendo Karuta by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Japan, Nintendo began by crafting traditional Japanese playing cards, especially hanafuda. The company's name, often interpreted as "leave luck to heaven," underscored its early direction.

1933–1968: Incorportation, expansion, and diversification

Collaborating with The Walt Disney Company in 1959, Nintendo entered the children's market and diversified its offerings, marking its transition into manufacturing games alongside playing cards.

1969–1972: Classic and electronic toys

The company established a dedicated department for electronic devices, led by Gunpei Yokoi, resulting in the creation of popular classic tabletop games such as chess, shogi, go, and mahjong.

2017 - Present days

The Nintendo Switch, a hybrid console combining home and handheld gaming, was released in 2017, featuring innovative Joy-Con controllers and a diverse game library from third-party developers. The company's expansion also extended beyond gaming, with the opening of Super Nintendo World at Universal Studios Japan in 2021 and the production of animated projects, including the highly successful Super Mario Bros. Movie in 2023.

Products

Nintendo Switch

The Nintendo Switch is a hybrid video game console, with the main unit inserted onto a docking station to connect to a television.

Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS is a handheld gaming console that offers 3D gaming without the need for special glasses.

The Super Mario Bros. game series

Featuring the adventures of Mario, a plumber turned hero, as he navigates through imaginative worlds to rescue Princess Peach from the clutches of the villainous Bowser, the series has defined platforming gameplay and cultural impact since its debut in 1985.
